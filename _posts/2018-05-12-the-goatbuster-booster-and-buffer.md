                                                                          width="300"/></a></div><h2>My First Effect:
    Booster and
    Buffer</h2>I was reading blogs and electronic books for a few months, when I decided I would go with my first pedal: a booster!
<br/>
<br/>As a rookie in electronic and soldering, I decided to go with a tutoriel from the Tonefiend blog archive:&nbsp;<a
        href="http://www.seymourduncan.com/tonefiend/tonefiend-diy-club-projects-resources/">http://www.seymourduncan.com/tonefiend/tonefiend-diy-club-projects-resources/</a>
<br/><br/>I went with project #3, the booster and buffer.<br/><br/>Here is the schematic from their document.<br/>

<br/>I replaced POT1 with a 330 ohm resistor, so the gain would be fixed (and clean). But you can leave the pot if you want to add gain (light drive) to your booster.
